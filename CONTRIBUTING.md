# Contributing to awesome-ai-film 🎬

Thank you for helping track the metamorphosis of filmmaking in the age of cheap intelligence.

This is a **curated** list — quality over quantity. Not every AI tool belongs here. Every entry should illuminate the *impossible → possible* transition.

## What Belongs Here

Ask yourself: **Does this item show us something about filmmaking that was literally impossible before AI?**

| ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
|--------|------|
| A tool that lets one person make a VFX shot that previously required a 50-person team | Yet another ChatGPT wrapper with "film" in the name |
| A paper that advances text-to-video coherence | A generic AI blog post with no technical substance |
| A film that uses AI in a way that changes the *kind* of story being told | A standard short film that happens to use an AI color grade |
| A philosophical essay that wrestles with what AI means for artistic intention | A hot take thread with no lasting insight |
| An economic analysis of what zero-cost production does to the industry | VC funding announcement for an AI video startup |

## Submission Guidelines

### Tools
- Must be **publicly available** (open source, commercial product, or beta with active waitlist)
- No vaporware, no "coming soon" landing pages with no product
- Link to the **product page**, not a press release or tweet
- Describe what it does in **one line** — focus on what makes it significant for filmmaking

### Papers
- Must have an **arXiv ID, DOI, or equivalent permanent identifier**
- Preprints welcome, but they must be publicly accessible
- One-line description should capture the **contribution**, not just the title

### Films & Examples
- Must be **viewable online** (link to the work itself)
- Describe what AI technique was used and **why the result matters**
- No "I prompted Midjourney and made a slideshow" — show genuine filmmaking craft

### Philosophy & Criticism
- Must be **substantive** — essays, books, lectures, published criticism
- Link to the original work, not commentary on commentary
- No hot takes, no Twitter threads (unless they sparked a genuine intellectual movement)

## How to Submit

1. **Fork** the repository
2. Add your item to the appropriate section, maintaining the existing format:
   - `- [Name](link) — One-line description.`
   - `- **Name** (Author/Creator, Year) — One-line description. [Source](link)`
3. Keep entries in **alphabetical order** within each section (by display name)
4. **One item per PR** unless they're thematically linked
5. Open a **Pull Request** with a title like: `Add [Tool Name] to AI Filmmaking Tools`

### PR Description Template

```markdown
## What
[Brief description of the item]

## Why it matters for the impossible → possible transition
[Explain what constraint this dissolves or what new capability it unlocks]

## Verification
- [ ] Publicly available (link works, not 404)
- [ ] Fits the section it's placed in
- [ ] One-line description is accurate and non-promotional
```

## Formatting Rules

- **Display name** as link text — not "Click here" or raw URLs
- **One line** per entry — no multi-paragraph descriptions
- **Oxford comma** in lists of three or more
- **Section headers** use `##` with Title Case
- **Subsection headers** use `###`
- No emoji in section headers (except the repo title)
- No marketing superlatives ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "best-ever")

## If You're Not Sure

Open an **Issue** first. Use the title: `[Proposal] [Item Name]`. Include:
- What the item is
- Which section it would go in
- Why it fits the "impossible → possible" theme

Discussion is welcome. This repo is as much a conversation as a list.
